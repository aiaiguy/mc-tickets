# MC Tickets — Strategy & Brand Direction

## Positioning in one line
The private concierge for people who don't queue. Your man on the inside for Melbourne's best seats.

## What the market looks like
- **Ticketverse**: Official AFL onseller. Corporate, web-first, transactional. Deep inventory, institutional tone, big sales team, published packages. They sell *access*.
- **The Golden Ticket**: Independent broker. Similar positioning, slightly more boutique. Still web-led.
- **Ticketmaster AFL Premium**: Official channel. Clean, cold, no relationship.

**The gap**: Nobody in Melbourne owns the "old school concierge" lane. Every competitor is a website with a cart and a contact form. MC Tickets operates on trust, relationships, WhatsApp, and a phone call with Mark. That is the product. The website's job is to prove legitimacy to new referrals, not to sell.

## Target personas

### 1. The Die-Hard Professional
Senior lawyer, surgeon, exec, founder. Loves their team. Hates Ticketek. Wants the best seat in the house for a Friday night blockbuster without opening an app at 9am on a Tuesday. Values: reliability, discretion, relationship.

### 2. The Corporate Host
Business development director, partner, sales leader. Entertains clients weekly. Needs Medallion Club, private suites, hospitality. Can't afford for a booking to go wrong in front of a client. Values: confidence, one call solves it, invoice later.

### 3. The Milestone Buyer
Husband, dad, mate. Birthday, anniversary, mate's 40th, kid's first game. Wants hero seats on a specific date and has no idea how to get them. Values: being the person who pulled it off.

Shared truth: **time-poor, cash-rich, allergic to friction**. They pay a premium for the answer being "yes".

## Brand principles
1. **Discretion over display.** Premium is quiet. No shouting, no stock photos, no badges.
2. **Relationship over transaction.** The site never pretends to be a store. Every CTA leads to a conversation with Mark.
3. **Insider over institution.** We're not the AFL. We're the guy the AFL guys call.
4. **Under-promise, over-deliver.** No hype. Confidence comes from tenure (10+ years, 1000+ members).

## Visual direction
- **Palette**: Near-black base, deep premium purple as hero accent, gold for value markers, silver for supporting text. Restrained. High contrast.
- **Type**: Serif display (Playfair Display) for headlines, clean sans (Inter) for body. Wide letter-spacing for labels.
- **Logo**: Circle-framed MC monogram + wordmark. Classical, elegant, no cleverness.
- **Motion**: Slow fade-ins on scroll. Subtle shimmer on the monogram. No bounces, no parallax gimmicks.
- **Imagery**: Almost none. Texture from gradients, typography, and negative space.

## Site architecture (v1)
1. Hero — monogram, tagline, single CTA (Request Access)
2. Positioning statement — one paragraph, big serif
3. How it works — 3 steps (Request → Curated → Delivered)
4. What we get you — category tiles (AFL, NRL/Origin, Concerts, F1, International, Grand Slam)
5. Sample inventory — marked "Example listings"
6. Why us — stats and trust signals
7. Membership request form — single form, routes to sales@mctickets.com.au
8. Footer — Mark Faber (holding), ABN placeholder, contact

## Copy rules
- Australian English
- No em dashes
- Short sentences
- Never "click here to buy"
- Always "request", "enquire", "speak to Mark"
- POA only

## Out of scope for v1

- Member login area
- WhatsApp bot (planned for v2)
